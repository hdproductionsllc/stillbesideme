Why this exists

When I lost my dog, people sent flowers. They sent cards. Every one of them ended up in the trash within a week — not because they didn't care, but because there's nothing to keep.

I wanted something that captured who he actually was. Not just his face, but the way he'd steal socks out of the laundry. The nickname only we used. The specific, weird, irreplaceable things that made him ours.

I couldn't find it. So I built it.

Every tribute we make starts with details only someone who loved that animal would know. The poem is written from those details. Then we print it alongside their photo in a frame that belongs on a wall for the rest of someone's life.

Flowers say "I'm sorry." This says "I remember who they were."
